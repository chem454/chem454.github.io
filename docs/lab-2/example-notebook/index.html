A custom function, calculateCI(), can be used to calculate the 95% confidence interval on the concentration.

The confidence interval on concentration determined from a standard curve is:

CI=tsCa

where t is the critical t value (3.182 for 3 degrees of freedom at the 95% CL) sCa is the standard deviation of the concentration, given by:

sCA=srka1m+1n+(S¯samp−S¯std)2(ka)2∑i=1n(Cstdi−C¯std)2

where m is the number of sample replicates, n is the number of standards, S¯ denotes the average signal for the samples and standards, C is the concentration of the standards, and s_r is the standard deviation about the regression:

sr=∑i=1n(yi−y^i)2n−2

such that the final concentration, Ca, is:

μCa=Ca±tsCa

The output of the function is CI (i.e. tSCa).
